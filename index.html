This website was developed as a finals submission for the BSCS-3A Course: Calculus Based Physics++. This website will be covering the following topics:

  • A live simulation of the Archimedes Principle - which states that the upward buoyant force on an object in a fluid is equal to the weight of the fluid that the object displaces. This principle explains why objects float or sink and is a fundamental concept in fluid mechanics used in applications like ship design and submarine operation.
  • Visuals for Bernoulli's Principle (airplane wing, fluid flow) - the principle in hydrodynamics that an increase in the velocity of a stream of fluid results in a decrease in pressure. Also called Bernoulli effect or Bernoulli theorem.
  • Practice Problems covering the related topics (Fluid Pressure, density, and bouyancy problems).
